The Project

The Incredible Shrinking Man is a multidisciplinary approach to a severe problem that affects all of us, climate change, which has been proven it is man-made, due to greenhouse gas emissions, which rised high not long after the Industrial Revolution.

Climate Change is already affecting us in significant ways, global temperature is rising to levels where previously iced areas are melting, leading to higher levels of sea which can destroy ecosystems, species, cities and people, higher global temperatures also influence these ecosystem destructions and biodiversity loss, which can only lead to global changes in migration habits, unpredictable and unseen severe weather, and further loss of prized resources for our planet

Several theoretical solutions have already been proposed, manipulating the weather, genetic engineering for withstanding of higher temperatures, bringing down greenhouse emissions, applying restrictions to companies and countries which go beyond limits... This is another solution, the shrinking of our current generation to a limit height, a way to force as little waste as possible with a minimal influence in everyday life for every of us.

We believe ours may be the most effective one for the short time span we hae for turning things around, medicine is a decently-developing field, brimming with talent, perseverance and a big community of people, and we want to give our help, at least, on starting to save the world

We also believe, do we ever find something, that not only the specialists and professionals should be the only to access this critical information on how the process of shrinkage is made, along with other very important knowledge. To solve this problem, our most important findings, highlights, and a full-on detailed insight of our research will be uploaded for free in WikiVersity, a repository of free original research and knowledge where anyone can contribute their small grain, learn more on how original research works and explore a potentially new concept of medicine. We want you to be an active part of it, because you deserve to know

Our team is Friederick Smithers, human anatomy specialist; Roman Retrovph, health consultant; Santiago Marcelo, bioengineer at Universidad de Malaga; Ricardo Velas, biostatitician; and Jacob Rutgen, biochemist.
